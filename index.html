1. Current System Assessment

Existing Warehouse & Aggregation System

Understanding the current state of operations, who uses the system, what data it captures, and how the producer-to-market linkage currently works.

📋 Current Scope

  • System: Warehouse & aggregation tracking (paper + basic digital records)
  • Primary users: Warehouse managers, aggregators, district agricultural officers
  • Coverage: 6 major districts — Kampala, Gulu, Mbale, Jinja, Masindi, Soroti
  • Commodities tracked: Coffee, maize, beans, sesame, cassava, groundnuts
  • Data format: Manual ledgers at aggregation points, spreadsheets at warehouses

🔗 Current Linkage Flow

  • Producer → Aggregation: Farmers deliver to nearest village aggregation point. No digital tracking of individual producer contributions.
  • Aggregation → Warehouse: Aggregated bulks are transported to district warehouses. Receive manually recorded.
  • Warehouse → Market: Stock released to primary markets on verbal or paper orders. Limited visibility of available stock.
  • Market → Trader: Traders purchase at market without visibility into upstream supply chain.

⚠️ Key Gaps

  • No producer identification — cannot trace commodity back to individual farmer
  • No real-time stock visibility across the chain
  • Price information is fragmented — farmers lack market price data
  • No geospatial mapping of production areas, aggregation points, or warehouses
  • Quality grading is inconsistent across aggregation centres
  • No linkage between producer registration and commodity flow

📌 What Needs to Change

  • Each producer must have a unique digital ID linked to their production
  • Each aggregation point, warehouse, and market must be geolocated
  • Commodity flow must be digitally tracked from farmer through to market sale
  • Real-time stock levels and prices must be visible to all authorised actors
  • Quality grading data must accompany every lot through the chain

3. Actors & Responsibilities

Who Does What

Every actor in the producer-to-market linkage has defined roles and data responsibilities within the system.

👨‍🌾 Farmer / Producer

Registers with system, receives unique ID. Reports production volumes, crop types, and harvest dates. Delivers to aggregation point and receives payment.

🏘️ Farmer Group

Organises smallholder members, coordinates collective delivery to aggregation point. Maintains group membership and consolidated production data.

🧑‍🌾 Extension Agent

Registers farmers, provides advisory on quality standards, monitors production estimates, verifies delivery records at aggregation points.

📦 Aggregator

Operates aggregation centre. Receives produce from farmers/groups, grades quality, weighs, records volumes. Issues digital receipt. Manages stock prior to warehouse transfer.

🏬 Warehouse Operator

Receives aggregated stock, issues warehouse receipt. Manages inventory, storage conditions, stock releases. Reports stock levels and movements in real time.

🏪 Market Operator

Manages primary market trading. Records commodities arriving, prices traded, trader transactions. Links warehouse releases to market sales.

🤝 Trader / Off-taker

Registered buyer on the platform. Views available stock at markets and warehouses. Purchases commodities, records transaction. Accessible for processing or resale.

🏛️ District Officer

Oversees aggregation and warehouse operations within district. Monitors volumes, prices, and compliance. Reports to national level.

📊 M&E Officer

Uses system dashboards to track value chain performance. Monitors producer participation, volumes moved, market access, and price trends.

7. Integration Points

External System Connections

This module does not operate in isolation. It must integrate with existing and planned national systems through defined APIs and data exchange protocols.

📝 National Registration System

Bi-directional sync of farmer, trader, and aggregator identities. Receive verified IDs, push registration data for new actors enrolled through this module.

💳 Payment System

Trigger payments to farmers upon delivery at aggregation point. Integrate with mobile money (MTN/Airtel) for direct producer settlements. Record payment transactions.

📊 Market Information System

Push real-time prices and volumes from primary markets. Receive market intelligence (price trends, demand data) to inform producers and aggregators.

🚚 Logistics System

Share transport requests from aggregation centres and warehouses. Receive fleet availability, track commodity movements, optimise collection routes.

📈 M&E Dashboard

Supply aggregated data on producer participation, volumes aggregated, warehouse occupancy, market throughput, and price trends for national monitoring.

🧾 Financial Inclusion

Share producer transaction history to support credit scoring. Enable linkage to agricultural finance products based on reliable production and sales data.

Producer-to-Primary Market Linkage Module — Part of the Agricultural Value Chain Information System (AV CIS). Designed to connect producers from village level through aggregation, warehousing, and primary markets using geospatial data, real-time tracking, and interoperable APIs.
